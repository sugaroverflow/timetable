### Topic: topic-knowledge-infrastructure

Title: Knowledge Infrastructure
Host: host-teddy
Status: published
Published date, if published: 2026-06-10
Cover image URL, if any:

Body:

There is a significant part of the scientific community who feels concerned about the future of science infrastructure. Things I include in science infrastructure are science institutions (universities, research organisations, companies), research facilities (labs, offices, buildings, equipment), talent (people), communication (conferences, journals, papers, peer review), and funding (grants, grant proposal writing, government funding). Knowledge infrastructure is about the communication part.

This is not about abstractly promoting communication and collaboration. This is about an absolutely vital and core component of how science works: unless we communicate effectively what knowledge we have discovered, we are bound to re-discover it. Maybe it's ok? Some inefficiency always exists. No! This is not about inefficiency: if we can't utilise what exists, we'll never be able to build on top of what others discover. Isolated discoveries mean no knowledge accumulation which leads to the breakdown of the global science machinery.

Many paths and approaches to fix this, open to discussion.

### Topic: topic-encrypted-communications

Title: Encrypted Communications
Host: host-teddy
Status: published
Published date, if published: 2026-06-10
Cover image URL, if any:

Body:

Encrypted communications is a fascinating topic in human history. With the advent of PGP in 1991 and more importantly Signal app (as Open Whisper Systems when it came out in 2013) we acquired an extremely powerful tool: provably secure and instant communications available at scale.

Western governments attempt to ban these tools every couple of years. ### Topic: topic-digital-deliberative-spaces

Published date, if published: 2026-06-10
Cover image URL, if any:

Body:

People collectively thinking, discussing, and deciding what they think about an issue is an important aspect of quasidemocratic polities. Ideally, deliberation enables peaceful synthesis of conflicting preferences and needs. Local assemblies are a simple yet dangerous tool to do this. Research, experimentation, and plurality are important to help make assemblies higher quality and hopefully more meaningfully useful in today's British society.
